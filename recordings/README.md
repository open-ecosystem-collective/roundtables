# Recordings

Every pitch from every Roundtable, recorded and published.

Each edition has its own subfolder with the three seven-minute clips
and their metadata. Partners are free to use their own clip wherever
it serves them.

## Why we record

So the room reaches further than the room. So partners can carry
their seven minutes wherever it serves them. So the public archive
keeps growing, edition by edition.

The promise of the Roundtables is that the 2026 conversations will
still be worth watching in 2028. This folder is how that promise
gets kept.

## What gets recorded

- The four-minute welcome and context
- The three lightning pitches (21 minutes total)
- The moderated Q&A (20 minutes)
- The five-minute wrap

Recording stops at 18:10, before the conversation after the formal
part begins. The two hours that follow are not on tape, by design.

## Folder structure

```
recordings/
├── README.md (this file)
├── 2026-Q1/
│   ├── README.md         (edition recording index)
│   ├── 00-welcome.md     (link + transcript notes for the welcome)
│   ├── 01-partner-1.md   (link + notes for partner 1's pitch)
│   ├── 02-partner-2.md
│   ├── 03-partner-3.md
│   ├── 04-qa.md          (link + notes for the Q&A)
│   └── 05-wrap.md
├── 2026-Q2/
└── ...
```

Each markdown file holds:
- A link to where the clip lives (YouTube, partner site, etc.)
- The pitch title
- The partner and their org
- One paragraph summary
- Any references mentioned during the pitch
- The transcript link (if one is published)

## Where the actual video lives

The repository hosts metadata, links, and summaries. The video files
themselves live on:

- The OEC YouTube channel (primary canonical home)
- Each partner's own channels (they keep their clip and post it
  wherever serves them)

The repository is the index. The platforms are the players.
