# Moderation Notes

> Notes for whoever holds the moderator's seat.

The default moderator is Tania (VRC). These notes exist so the role
can pass cleanly when needed, and so the format keeps its shape
regardless of who is holding the microphone.

## Before the evening

**Two weeks out:**
- Confirm the three voices, the venue host, and the date with all parties
- Send each partner the [partner brief](partner-brief.md) if they have
  not seen it
- Ask each partner for: pitch title, one-line summary, one sentence on
  what they want the room to take away
- Open the meetup RSVP

**One week out:**
- Three pitch prep calls (15-30 min each), unless the partner declines
- Confirm the venue, the food and drinks plan, any merch logistics
- Share the venue address with confirmed RSVPs only, never publicly

**Day of:**
- Arrive at the venue by 16:00 for setup
- Test recording equipment with each partner before 17:00
- Print three name tents (partner names on the seat)
- Three reserve questions written, on paper, in the moderator's pocket

## The reserve questions

The three reserve questions exist so the room never goes silent during
the Q&A. They are written on the day of, after the pitches arrive, and
discarded if not needed.

Good reserve questions:
- Pull on a thread shared between two pitches
- Ask one partner to react to what another said
- Open a tension the pitches sidestepped politely

Bad reserve questions:
- Ones that could have been written a week ago
- Ones that ask each partner the same thing in turn
- Ones designed to get a sound bite

## During the evening

**17:00 - The threshold**
- Stand near the door for the first ten minutes
- Make sure each partner has a drink before the welcome
- Watch for guests who do not know anyone yet, introduce them around

**17:15 - The welcome**
- Four minutes. Keep it tight. The room came for the pitches.
- Name the housekeeping items (bathrooms, food, when the formal part wraps)
- Read the three partner intros from your notes, not from memory
- The "educational, not sales" reminder is one sentence, not a paragraph

**17:20 - The pitches**
- Keep time. At 6:30 give a quiet signal (raised hand, no words).
- At 7:00 the next voice begins, even if the previous is mid-thought.
- Do not introduce the next partner mid-pitch. The transition is silence.
- After the third pitch, take 30 seconds before the Q&A. Let the room breathe.

**17:45 - The Q&A**
- Open questions first. Wait. The first question is always the slowest.
- When the second question comes, pull at the thread between it and the first.
- Hold the reserve questions until the room runs out of its own.
- It rarely does by then.

**18:05 - The wrap-up**
- One takeaway per voice, in order: Partner 1, Partner 2, Partner 3
- The teaser for next time should name dates, not partners (partners
  may shift)
- The feedback reminder is one line: "drop a note on Meetup or GitHub"

**18:10 - Recording off**
- Announce that recording is stopping
- Thank the room
- Step off the microphone

## After the evening

- Recording goes to the editor within 48 hours
- Each partner gets their own seven-minute clip within two weeks
- Edition entry on GitHub goes up within a week
  ([template](../editions/_template.md))
- Reserve question paper is thrown away, not archived

## When something goes wrong

**A partner cancels last minute:**
- Two-voice Roundtable is fine. Do not scramble for a replacement.
- Adjust the pitch window: 14 minutes for two voices, 7 each.
- The Q&A absorbs the extra time.

**The room is dead during Q&A:**
- Reserve question one. Wait. Then reserve question two.
- If still silent, end the Q&A early. The conversation after the formal
  part will pick up what the room held back.

**A pitch runs long:**
- Quiet signal at 6:30 stays quiet. No words from the moderator.
- At 7:00, stand up. Most pitchers will land within 30 seconds.
- At 7:30, interrupt politely. "Thank you, we will pick that up in the Q&A."

**Tension between two partners on stage:**
- Let it sit. Do not deflect.
- This is one of the moments worth recording.
- The room came for honesty. Trust it.

## When to break the format

Almost never. The format is the format because it survives the
seasons. If you find yourself wanting to break it, ask first whether
it is a one-evening problem or a format-level problem.

One-evening problems get solved on the night.
Format-level problems get raised in a feedback issue, after.
