# The Shape of an Evening

> One formal hour. Two of real conversation.

The 17:00 to 20:00 window is on purpose. Late enough to fit inside a
working day. Early enough to leave the evening intact. Long enough to
give the part that matters the time it actually needs.

## Timeline

| #  | Time  | Duration    | Phase                          |
|----|-------|-------------|--------------------------------|
| 01 | 17:00 | arrival     | Threshold                      |
| 02 | 17:15 | 4 min       | Welcome & context              |
| 03 | 17:20 | 21 min      | Lightning pitches              |
| 04 | 17:45 | 20 min      | Discussion & Q&A               |
| 05 | 18:05 | 5 min       | Wrap-up & next steps           |
| 06 | 18:10 | until 20:00 | Food, drinks, conversation     |

## 01 · 17:00 - Threshold

Name tag, drink in hand, a few minutes to find a face you know or
one you have been meaning to.

The first fifteen minutes are unstructured by design. Some people show
up at 17:00 sharp, some at 17:13. Both are fine. Nobody is on stage yet.

## 02 · 17:15 - Welcome & context (4 min)

The moderator opens the evening.

- Welcome and housekeeping (where the bathrooms are, where the food
  will be, when the formal part wraps)
- The agenda for the evening
- Brief mention of the three partners on stage tonight
- For each: name, role in the ecosystem, time in the ecosystem
- A reminder that this is an educational space, not a sales one

## 03 · 17:20 - Lightning pitches (21 min)

Seven minutes per voice. Recorded. The point is to say what is true,
not to fit every slide.

- **Partner 1** - max. 7 minutes
- **Partner 2** - max. 7 minutes
- **Partner 3** - max. 7 minutes

The moderator keeps time. At 6:30 a quiet signal. At 7:00 the next
voice begins.

Slides are optional, not encouraged. The voice carries further when
there is nothing to read along to.

## 04 · 17:45 - Discussion & Q&A (20 min)

A moderated conversation that pulls on the threads from the pitches.

- Open questions from the room first
- The moderator weaves a thread between the three pitches
- Three questions held in reserve, in case the room is quiet
- It rarely is by then

The reserve questions exist so the room never goes silent. They are
written after the pitches arrive, on the day of, and discarded if not
needed. They are not a script.

## 05 · 18:05 - Wrap-up & next steps (5 min)

- One key takeaway per voice
- **Partner 1** - what one thing you want the room to keep
- **Partner 2** - same
- **Partner 3** - same
- A short word about who comes next time
- A reminder to drop feedback, so the format keeps growing in the
  direction the community wants it to

## 06 · 18:10 to 20:00 - Food, drinks, conversation

The formal part is done. The reason most people came is the part
that follows. Food. More drinks. The conversations that only happen
once the stage is empty.

Two clean hours. Long enough to actually finish a thought. Short
enough that it does not eat the whole evening.

## Notes on timing

The window is firm at the start and soft at the end. 17:00 is when
the door opens, not when the formal part begins. The formal part
ends at 18:10, on the dot, even if the room is humming. The
conversation continues without the moderator on a microphone.

Recording stops at 18:10. What is said after is not on tape, by design.
