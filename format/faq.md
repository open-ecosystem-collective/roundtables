# FAQ

> The questions that keep coming up.

## On the format

### Why three voices and not five?

Three is enough to make a conversation. More than three and the
pitches start to feel like a parade. The Q&A also gets thinner the
more voices are on stage, because the room has to spread its attention.

### Why seven minutes?

Long enough to land one story with one lesson. Short enough that
people prepare seriously, because they cannot hide behind padding.

### Why quarterly?

Conferences come once a year, and the rooms are too big to learn
anyone's name. A monthly meetup turns into an obligation. Quarterly
is the rhythm where the same faces start to recognise each other
without it becoming a job.

### Why Zurich?

The DACH cloud-native scene has a centre of gravity here. Easy to
reach from Bern, Basel, Geneva, Munich, Stuttgart, Milan. The
Roundtables may travel to other cities later, but the home stays here.

### Why in person?

A virtual format would reach more people, and we may run one later
for that reason. But the part of the evening that matters most -
the conversation after the formal part - does not work over a video
call. The two hours after 18:10 are the reason for the whole format.

### Why no sponsorship fee?

Money introduces the wrong gravity. Once a partner pays for a stage,
the conversation tilts toward what they want said. The Roundtables
work because the only thing partners pay for is their share of the
food and drinks. The investment is presence, not budget.

## On being a partner

### Can my company sponsor the Roundtables?

No, not in the usual sponsorship sense. Companies can:
- Be invited as a voice on stage
- Offer to host an evening at their venue
- Bring customers when on stage

What companies cannot do:
- Pay to be on stage
- Pay to have their logo on the page
- Buy guaranteed slots in advance

### What is the cost?

For partners on stage: your one-third share of food, drinks, and any
merch for the evening. The exact amount is shared with the partners
once the cast is confirmed, and varies with the venue and the room size.
A rough budget is in the [partner brief](partner-brief.md).

### Can I bring slides?

Yes, but three slides is plenty. Most of the best pitches use zero.

### Can I bring my own customers?

Yes, this is one of the contributions a partner brings. Five to eight
people you personally invite, with a personal note. The room is
composed by hand, not by registration form.

### What happens if I have to cancel?

Tell us as soon as you know. Two voices on stage is a fine
Roundtable. We do not scramble for a replacement.

### Do I get the recording?

Yes. Each partner gets their own seven-minute clip, edited and
ready to use, within two weeks of the evening.

You can put it on your website, in a careers post, in a customer
presentation, anywhere it serves you. The clip is yours.

## On attending

### How do I get on the guest list?

[Join the Meetup group](https://meetup.com/open-ecosystem-collective).
Each Roundtable opens RSVPs there. Guest list is capped, first
RSVPs in, first served.

### Where is the venue?

The address travels with the RSVP, not with the invitation. Once
your spot is confirmed on Meetup, you get the address.

### Is the event in English or German?

English. Most of the DACH cloud-native scene works in English already.

### Do I have to come for the whole evening?

The formal part is 17:00 to 18:10. After that, the room is loose.
You can leave whenever. We hope you do not, because the after-part is
the actual point, but the door is unlocked.

### Is there food?

Yes. The three partners on stage jointly bring food and drinks for
the room. Vegetarian and vegan options always. Allergy notes can go
in your Meetup RSVP.

## On the recordings

### Are the recordings public?

Yes, after editing. Each pitch goes up on the
[recordings/](../recordings/) folder of this repository, with
links to wherever the partners publish their clip on their own
channels.

### Can I opt out of being recorded?

The three voices on stage and the moderator are on tape. Everyone
else in the room is not. The recording stops at 18:10, before the
conversation starts.

If you are concerned about being audible in the background of a
pitch recording, sit further back. The audio captures the stage,
not the room.

### Where do the recordings live long-term?

This repository, under [recordings/](../recordings/), with mirror
links to YouTube and the partners' own channels.

The point of the archive is that the Roundtables of 2026 will
still be worth watching in 2028. The repository is the canonical
location.

## On the bigger picture

### How is this different from a meetup?

Meetups are usually one-format, one-host, one-channel. The Roundtables
are designed as a public archive first, a community evening second.
The recording, the editions index, the partner brief - all of that
exists because the format is meant to outlive any single evening.

### How is this different from a conference?

A conference is once a year, big room, dozens of voices, marketing
booth in the lobby. A Roundtable is four times a year, small room,
three voices, no booth, no logos.

Conferences are how an ecosystem announces itself. Roundtables
are how it gets to know itself.

### How does this connect to the rest of the OEC?

The [Open Ecosystem Collective](https://github.com/open-ecosystem-collective)
publishes a methodology for partner ecosystem management. The
methodology is the **what** and the **how** of running partnerships.
The Roundtables are where the methodology gets faces and the partner
landscape gets voices.

In a sentence: the methodology repo is for partner managers, the
Roundtables are for everyone who builds a partner ecosystem in DACH.
